## Project Overview

This project is based on understanding digital literacy and how important it is in today’s student life. As part of this project , I worked as a “Student Digital Ambassador,” where my role was to explore different digital tools and learn how to use them in a safe and responsible way.

In today’s time, most of our work — whether it is studying, communicating, or submitting assignments — depends on digital platforms. Through this project, I got the opportunity to not only learn about these tools but also use them practically. Each task in this project focused on a different aspect of digital literacy, such as awareness, online presence, communication, and cyber safety.

Overall, this project helped me understand how to be more responsible and confident while using digital technologies.



## Task 1: Digital Literacy Infographic

For this task, I created an infographic using Canva. The main goal was to spread awareness about digital literacy among students. In my design, I included topics like what digital literacy means, why it is important, some basic internet safety tips, and useful tools that students can use in their daily academic work.

While making the infographic, I tried to keep the design simple so that anyone can easily understand it. I also used icons and colors to make it visually appealing. This task was interesting because it allowed me to be creative while also sharing useful information.

 Folder: task-1-presentation/

## Task 2: Digital Portfolio

In this task, I created and updated my profiles on platforms like GitHub, LinkedIn, and Kaggle. Before this, I had very little idea about maintaining a professional online presence. While working on this task, I learned how these platforms are used by students and professionals to showcase their skills and achievements.

I added my basic details and educational information and understood how these profiles can help in the future for internships and career opportunities. This task made me realize that building a digital identity from the beginning is very important.

 Folder: task-2-portfolio/

## Task 3: Platforms Exploration

This task was divided into two parts. In the first part, I explored a coding platform and solved a beginner-level problem. It helped me understand basic problem-solving and gave me confidence to start coding.

In the second part, I created a Google Form quiz based on digital literacy. The form included different types of questions like multiple choice and short answers. I also checked the responses using Google Sheets, which helped me understand how data is collected and managed.

This task was useful because it introduced me to both technical and collaborative tools that I can use in my academic work.

## Task 4: Email Etiquette

In this task, I learned how to write professional emails. I wrote two emails — one to a professor requesting an extension and another to apply for an internship.

Earlier, I didn’t pay much attention to how emails should be written, but through this task, I understood the importance of proper structure, polite language, and clarity. I also created a checklist of do’s and don’ts for social media usage, which helped me understand responsible online behavior.

This task improved my communication skills and made me more aware of how to present myself professionally.

Folder: task-4-email-etiquette/

## Task 5: Cybercrime Awareness

For this task, I worked on a case study related to phishing, which is a common type of cybercrime. I learned how attackers try to trick people into sharing personal information through fake messages and emails.

While researching this topic, I realized how important it is to stay alert while using the internet. I also created a prevention checklist with simple steps that students can follow to stay safe online.

This task made me more careful and aware of online risks, and it will definitely help me avoid such situations in the future.

 Folder: task-5-cybercrime/
